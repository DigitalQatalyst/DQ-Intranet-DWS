DWS | AGILE 6xD PAGE SPECIFICATION

1. Purpose
The Agile 6xD page explains how DQ turns digital transformation from theory into execution. It positions Agile 6xD as DQ’s execution system—a practical, applied way to move from understanding problems to delivering real outcomes through work. The page follows a narrative: Problem → Execution System → Perspectives → Application → Real Use at DQ.

2. Target Users
- New joiners learning how DQ executes transformation
- Existing associates seeking clarity on how DQ works
- Practitioners applying Agile 6xD in real work
- Leaders seeking clarity on execution (not theory)

3. Page Structure & Story-Based Sections

3.1 Hero Section
- Story role: Introduce the core problem and immediately position Agile 6xD as the answer.
- Purpose: Establish why digital transformation fails; frame execution as the missing piece.
- Content: Strong problem-led headline; one-line explanation of Agile 6xD as an execution system; primary CTA.
- Functionality: CTA links to the Agile 6xD Storybook; no operational actions.

3.2 What Is Agile 6xD?
- Story role: Shift from problem to understanding the execution system.
- Purpose: Explain Agile 6xD at a high level; show the balance between thinking and doing.
- Content: Section title; short explanation; two concept cards — Making Sense (understanding complexity and context) and Practical Execution (turning clarity into real work); CTA to read the full Storybook.
- Functionality: Informational; CTA links to detailed Agile 6xD documentation.

3.3 Six Perspectives. One Execution System.
- Story role: Show how execution is structured, not random.
- Purpose: Explain how Agile 6xD removes execution blockers; introduce the six perspectives as delivery lenses.
- Content: Section title; short description; perspective list — Digital Economy; Digital Cognitive Organisation; Digital Business Platforms; Digital Transformation 2.0; Digital Worker & Workspace; Digital Accelerators; active perspective detail card with description and link.
- Functionality: Users can switch perspectives; each perspective links to Knowledge Center content; reinforces structured execution.

3.4 Bring It to Life
- Story role: Move from understanding to action.
- Purpose: Show how Agile 6xD is applied, not just learned.
- Content: Section title; short supporting line; action cards — Learn by Doing (Learning Center), Knowledge Center, Storybook.
- Functionality: Cards link to practical learning and reference material; encourages hands-on execution.

3.5 Agile 6xD Inside DQ
- Story role: Prove the system is real and already in use.
- Purpose: Show Agile 6xD running inside DQ first; connect internal execution to deployable products.
- Content: Section title; short explanation of internal application; CTA buttons.
- Functionality: CTAs link to Agile 6xD Storybook and Agile 6xD Products; bridges execution to scale.

3.6 Footer
- Story role: Provide continuity and navigation.
- Purpose: Support exploration beyond this page.
- Content: DQ Digital Workspace branding; navigation links; legal and version information.
